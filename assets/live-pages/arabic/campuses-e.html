Clayton

www.monash.edu/campuses/clayton

Clayton is our first and largest campus – in fact it's so big, it's got its own postcode.

It's the hub of the University's research, and has a life and energy outside its education programs. The campus sporting facilities are excellent, and the Australian football team – the Socceroos – trained here in preparation for the 2009 Asian Cup and 2010 World Cup.

  • 20 km from Melbourne
  • 26,689 students from 105 countries
  • Home to a major biomedical research zone, the world's largest robotic crystallization facility, one of the world's most powerful electron microscopes, and the Southern Hemisphere's major wind tunnel. The Australian Synchrotron and a major research and development site of the Commonwealth Scientific and Industrial Research Organisation (CSIRO) adjoin the campus
  • Clayton is one of the few university campuses in Australia to accommodate a freestanding Religious Centre. It comprises a large worship and meeting area for the use of all religious groups on campus. It also houses prayer rooms equipped with ablution facilities, prayer mats and slippers
  • A mosque is located on Beddoe Avenue, directly adjacent to the campus, with separate washing facilities for men and women
  • Course options: single and double bachelor and honours degrees, diplomas, postgraduate coursework degrees, advanced research degrees, and PhDs
  • Faculties represented on the campus: Arts, Business and Economics; Education; Engineering; Information Technology; Law; Medicine, Nursing and Health Sciences; and Science

Caulfield

www.monash.edu/campuses/caulfield

Close to the centre of Melbourne, Caulfield offers excellent student facilities, and is complemented by a selection of nearby restaurants.

It's serviced by the Caulfield railway station, one of Melbourne's main suburban transport junctions, meaning access to the campus is incredibly easy, not only by train but by tram and bus as well.

  • 9 km from Melbourne
  • 14,328 students from 98 countries
  • Adjacent to Caulfield train station, tram and bus services
  • There are two Muslim prayer rooms on campus, one for men, and the other for women
  • Course options: single and double bachelor and honours degrees, diplomas, postgraduate coursework degrees, advanced research degrees, and PhDs
  • Faculties represented on the campus: Art and Design; Arts; Business and Economics; Information Technology; and Medicine, Nursing and Health Sciences

Parkville

www.pharm.monash.edu

Just three kilometres from the centre of Melbourne, Parkville offers modern, innovative and interactive facilities.

It's home to the Faculty of Pharmacy and Pharmaceutical Sciences and is situated within the prestigious 'Parkville Strip', the focal point for biomedical knowledge and research in Melbourne.

It's also around the corner from the multicultural hubs of Lygon Street and Sydney Road with their cafes, restaurants and plentiful entertainment options.

  • 1596 students from 38 countries
  • First-class teaching facilities and research laboratories following a recent A$45 million redevelopment
  • There is a Muslim prayer room on campus
  • Course options: single bachelor degrees, advanced research degrees, and PhDs
  • Faculties represented on the campus: Pharmacy and Pharmaceutical Sciences

Peninsula

www.monash.edu/campuses/peninsula

Located in the bayside suburb of Frankston, the Peninsula campus provides a warm and friendly study environment. Towering gum trees and beautiful ferns give the setting a distinctly Australian flavour.

It offers excellent on-campus accommodation and the popular beaches of Port Phillip Bay and the Mornington Peninsula are just down the road.

  • 45 km from Melbourne
  • 3915 students from 41 countries
  • There is a Muslim prayer room on campus
  • Course options: single and double bachelor and honours degrees, diplomas, postgraduate coursework degrees, advanced research degrees, and PhDs
  • Faculties represented on the campus: Arts; Business and Economics; Education; and Medicine, Nursing and Health Science
 

Berwick

www.berwick.monash.edu.au

The suburb of Berwick has the feel of a small country town, but is well connected to the city of Melbourne by train and freeways.

The main street, a short walk from our friendly and welcoming campus, accommodates inviting cafes and shops.

  • 40 km from Melbourne
  • 1994 students from 36 countries
  • Accessible by train, bus and car
  • There is a quiet room (for reflection and prayer) on campus
  • Course options: single and double bachelor and honours degrees, diplomas, postgraduate coursework degrees and PhDs
  • Faculties represented on the campus: Arts, Business and Economics, and Information Technology

Gippsland

www.gippsland.monash.edu.au

Situated on 63 hectares of landscaped grounds, Gippsland is our regional Victorian campus. It offers space, tranquillity and purpose-built facilities of the highest standard.

It's also located in one of Victoria's most diverse regions – offering easy access to snowfields, mountains and national parks. On campus, students can find a golf course, swimming pool and gym facilities.

  • 160 km from Melbourne (about a two-hour drive)
  • 2000 on-campus students and around 5000 off-campus students from 61 countries
  • There is a multi-faith prayer and reflection room on campus
  • Course options: single and double bachelor and honours degrees, diplomas, postgraduate coursework degrees, advanced research degrees, and PhDs
  • Faculties represented on the campus: Art and Design; Arts; Business and Economics; Education; Information Technology; Medicine, Nursing and Health Sciences; and Science

Sunway, Malaysia

www.monash.edu.my/advancement
/future

State-of-the-art teaching and learning infrastructure within an A$80 million purpose-built complex, puts Sunway campus, Kuala Lumpur, in the top echelon of Asian universities.

Studying at Sunway lets students experience fun, adventure and independence.

  • 4807 students from 52 countries
  • 40 fully-equipped science laboratories, including state-of-the-art physical containment lab for experimentation in genetic manipulation of DNA
  • 24 specialised engineering laboratories dedicated to robotics, thermodynamics, mechatronics and fluid mechanics, as well as an electron microscope facility
  • Malaysia's only live cell imaging microscope and single cell imaging microscope
  • Specific medicine and health science laboratories dedicated to cancer research, toxicology, infectious disease, epidemiology and brain research
  • Course options: single and double bachelors and honours degrees, diplomas, postgraduate coursework degrees, advanced research degrees, and PhDs
  • Schools represented on the campus: Arts and Social Sciences; Business; Engineering; Information Technology; Medicine and Health Sciences; and Science

South Africa

www.monash.ac.za

The Monash South Africa campus is modern and fast-growing, just 23 kilometres from the thriving commercial capital of South Africa, Johannesburg.

Students attending this campus enjoy small classes, advanced facilities and state-of-the-art technology. The campus has a strong volunteering ethos that sees many students giving up their free time to help disadvantaged school children.

  • 25 km from the Johannesburg city centre
  • 2166 students from 45 countries
  • 100-hectare site offers advanced facilities and state-of-the-art technology
  • Course options: bachelors and honours degrees
  • Faculties represented on the campus: Arts; Business and Economics; and Information Technology

Prato Centre, Italy

www.ita.monash.edu

Monash University's Prato Centre is located in Tuscany, Italy. It hosts short or semester-long courses, as well as workshops, seminars and conferences.

  • Located near the Renaissance city of Florence
  • Hosts Monash students completing short courses and semester-length subjects as part of their degree
  • Seeks to develop and expand Australia's connections with universities, governments, cultural organisations and industry in Europe
  • Venue for international workshops, seminars and conferences
