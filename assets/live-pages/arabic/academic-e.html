We are also proud to employ four Prime Minister's University Teacher of the Year Award winners, one of whom is Professor Stephen Barkoczy.

Professor Stephen Barkoczy

An expert in taxation law, superannuation law and venture capital law, Professor Stephen Barkoczy is a prime example of the dedication and expertise of our educators.

"I make an effort to bring the issues I come across through my consulting and researching into the classroom. In my advanced tax classes, we often work from the documents back to the law rather than concentrate purely on theory. I try to make it real for the students and get them to think laterally and creatively about how the law is applied," Professor Barkoczy said.

Nobel Prize Winners

2007 Nobel Peace Prize

Eight scientists from Monash University shared in the prestige of the 2007 Nobel Peace Prize.

The prize was awarded to members of the Intergovernmental Panel on Climate Change (IPCC) and environment campaigner Al Gore "for their efforts to build up and disseminate greater knowledge about man-made climate change, and to lay the foundations for the measures that are needed to counteract such change."

2009 Nobel Prize for Physiology/Medicine

Professor Elizabeth Blackburn is one of the most distinguished molecular biologists in the world. She was awarded the 2009 Nobel Prize for Physiology or Medicine, along with Carol Greider and Jack Szostak of the US, for discovering an enzyme that helps chromosomes in cells stay eternally young. These discoveries have direct implications for the treatment of diseases such as cancer.

Professor Blackburn is a Sir Louis Matheson Distinguished Visiting Professor in the Monash University School of Biomedical Sciences.

She is a regular visitor to Monash and contributes to the life of the school through student lectures, mentoring and research advice. She has played a vital role inspiring early career researchers and female scientists in Australia and across the world.

Professor Blackburn is Australia's eleventh – and the country's first female – Nobel Prize winner. Among her awards are the Lasker Prize for Medical Research (2006) and the Gruber Prize for Genetics.

In 2009 Professor Blackburn was listed among Time Magazine's 100 Most Influential People in The World.
